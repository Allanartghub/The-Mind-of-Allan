MindSprings — AI‑Powered Mental Wellness Companion

A team-built mobile app (Citi Upstart Challenge) that helps students understand and manage their emotional wellbeing through emoji mood check‑ins, gentle journaling, an AI companion, and a supportive community space.

Tools

Overview

MindSpring addresses a gap in student wellbeing tools by combining quick mood check‑ins, reflective journaling (text or voice), and an approachable AI chat that encourages healthy habits — all wrapped in a privacy‑first design.

This page is a lightweight first pass. We’ll iterate with richer visuals, user flows, and research artifacts.

My Objectives

  • Design an MVP that feels emotionally safe and non‑clinical.
  • Prototype key flows for check‑ins, journaling, and AI guidance.
  • Validate desirability and usability with student testers.

The Problem

Students struggle to maintain consistent self‑care practices. Existing apps often feel generic, clinical, or noisy, leading to low engagement and drop‑off.

The Solution

MindSpring offers a gentle, structured routine: quick emoji check‑ins, low‑friction journal/voice entries, an AI companion for reflection, and a community corner for peer support — with transparent data choices.

Minimum Viable Product

Emoji Mood Check‑Ins

Daily taps build a lightweight habit and generate a personal mood timeline.

Journal & Voice Notes

Private space to reflect with optional prompts; voice for low‑effort capture.

AI Companion

Supportive chat that mirrors feelings, surfaces patterns, and suggests next steps.

Wellness Exercises

Short breathing, grounding, and gratitude practices tied to mood state.

Community Corner

Opt‑in peer space with moderation and anonymized sharing.

Consent & Privacy

Clear onboarding, data choices, and content controls build trust.

Design Process

  1. Research: Interviews and competitive scan; themes: burnout, loneliness, privacy concerns.
  2. Define: JTBD and problem statement aligned to emotional safety.
  3. Ideate: Flows for check‑ins, prompts, and gentle nudge system.
  4. Prototype: Figma interactive prototype → usability testing → iteration.
  5. Build: React Native prototype with Supabase; mock AI for staged testing.

What I Learned

  • Emotional safety is a design requirement, not an afterthought.
  • Transparent AI behavior and data controls build trust.
  • Short, contextual nudges beat generic reminders.
